Two types of carers

Primary carers:

Those responsible for most of a person's everyday care. (often like a full-time job at home).


Secondary carers:

Those who assist with care but do not hold primary responsibility.

What happened?

When primary carers provide under 20 hours of care, their employment rate is high at 71.6%, but this falls to 45.4% when caregiving exceeds 40 hours.

So, What Does This Tell Us?

It is hard to ignore that in Australia, women make up the majority of primary carers, often working long unpaid hours at home while facing reduced employment opportunities.
This gender inequality in unpaid care work is shaped by society's deep gendered assumptions, leaving people like Mia to carry responsibilities that it has yet to fairly recognise or support.
